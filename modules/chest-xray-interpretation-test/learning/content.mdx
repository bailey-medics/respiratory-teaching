# Chest X-ray Interpretation

## What this module covers

An introduction to systematic chest X-ray interpretation:

- The ABCDE approach to reading chest radiographs
- Common pathological findings and their appearances
- Clinical correlation and when to escalate

## Systematic approach

Always review a chest X-ray using a structured method to avoid
missing subtle findings.

The **ABCDE** mnemonic:

- **A**irway — trachea, carina, main bronchi
- **B**ones — ribs, clavicles, spine, scapulae
- **C**ardiac — heart size, borders, mediastinal contour
- **D**iaphragm — costophrenic angles, free gas
- **E**verything else — soft tissues, lines/tubes, review areas

## Key teaching point

<Callout type="info">
  Always compare with previous imaging when available. A new finding
  on a single film may represent normal variant or chronic change —
  comparison helps distinguish acute pathology.
</Callout>
